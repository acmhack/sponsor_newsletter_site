FAQ

What payment options are there?
  • We have 2 payment options. Option # 1 is sending a check. Option #2 is using a credit card/debit card through our Missouri S&T ACM Stripe account. However, this option includes an additional transaction fee of 2.2% + 30 cents.
What does mentorship look like? Do mentors have to stay the entire time?
  • Mentorship can look like a lot of things, but the only thing we ask of you is to not do the project for them. We want to encourage learning and growth. Teach them about a concept they might not fully understand or help them get past a road block they're having!
  • Building a project has two major components:
    • The first being the actual development of the project. More technical mentors will thrive in this area.
    • The second part is the preparation for demoing, which can make or break even the best project. More business driven mentors will find this to be their area of expertise.
How does judging work?
  • Groups of judges will visit a list of teams, similar to a science fair, and score/rank those projects using a scorecard. We will then collect those scorecards and tally the results. Pending time constraints, the top X number of teams will give a final demo during closing ceremonies and winners will be revealed.*
  • *This might seem vague, and it is meant to be. Not everything will be solidified until much closer to the time of judging once we have total numbers of judges and projects. Judges will be briefed the morning of with exact assignments and instructions.
  • For sponsors with a custom prompt: You will be responsible for judging those who tag their projects with your prompt. Don't worry we will help make sure you know who they are and where they are! These will also be announced during closing ceremonies!
When do we need to be there by?
  • Please be at the venue 1-2 hours before check-in opens to give you ample amount of time to set up. Right after checkin in is a popular time for students/hackers to go around and meet the sponsors at their booths.
How long should we be at the booth/venue?
  • There are ideal times to be at your booth: mini career fair, before and after events, and meals (passing periods)
  • We definitely encourage you go out and meet the students where they're working too. Members of our team will also be available throughout the weekend to help connect teams to sponsors.
What is swag? How much should we bring?
  • Swag can be all sorts of things: shirts, stickers, water bottles, bags, etc.
  • Some advice we have to offer in this area: quality over quantity is huge. Students are much more likely to use / showcase one item of great use than a bunch of items that might not be the most useful.
    • A great example of this last year was from Software Design Partners, who gave out shirts that we see students wearing in our classes to this day.
What is a custom prize?
  • A custom prize is an opportunity for sponsors (Silver Tier+) to bring their own prompt that students can submit their projects to. These prompts can range from using certain tools and/or building certain projects.
  • An example of our favorite examples of a custom prompt in the past was Panera offering internships to the team with "Best Nutrition Hack", a project relating to nutrition and fitness.
  • To find more examples, check out our devpost from 2019:
    https://pickhacks.devpost.com/
What is a mini event?
  • A mini event is a perk of sponsors (Gold Tier+), who have the opportunity to host a 1-hour event of their choosing. Examples of past mini events include: tech talks, workshops, panels, games, etc.
What is the dress code / is there one?
  • There is no official dress code. All we ask is that you are presentable :). People typically wear jeans and a company shirt!
What should our social media posts look like? / Time frame it'll go out
  • You can check out all of our previous social media posts at @sandtpickhacks! They typically get published 2-3 weeks before the event!
    • Things we need: custom small blurb, photos, and an optional short clip!
